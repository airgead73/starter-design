Brainstorm Your Ideas

Brainstorming is any form of writing or drawing that allows you to generate ideas. Brainstorming is particularly useful if you do not have a clear idea of what you want to write. The object is to generate as many ideas as possible so that you can find possible directions for your paper.

Start by writing down the topic you’d like to write about. Then write down any ideas that come to mind about the topic. The quantity of ideas is more important than their quality—the more ideas you come up with, the more you have to choose from.

Don’t censor any ideas. Wait until the end of the process to eliminate bad ideas.

Go ahead and consider wild ideas. You can toss them later if they don’t work, but wild ideas can sometimes spark creative ideas that will. Feel free to combine ideas to come up with new, maybe improved, ideas.After you’ve generated as many ideas as you can think of, start narrowing them down. Toss those that you think are unworkable, do not interest you, do not fit well with the other ideas, or will be difficult to cover within the scope of your paper.

Tips

You can brainstorm by yourself, with another person, or with a small group of people. Often, others will think of ideas for your paper that you might not have discovered on your own.

Save the ideas you brainstormed, even if you don’t use them all. As you write and revise your paper, you may find you want to use one or two of the ideas you rejected initially. Or if you are stuck in your writing, one of your rejected ideas may spark more ideas that can help you continue with your paper.

Resources

There are several online mind-mapping tools that can help you brainstorm, including these free ones:

  1. With Coggle, you have immediate access to a visual representation of your ideas. If you like to collaborate, you can invite others to contribute to your mind map as you brainstorm.
  2. Popplet creates less artistic but more cleanly designed mind maps. If you like organization and dislike clutter, Popplet may be the best tool for you. This tool works well on mobile devices and tablets.
  3. If you live your life in the Google Cloud, you can download MindMup 2. This tool gets a lot of praise from users because it is simple, intuitive, and easy to learn.
